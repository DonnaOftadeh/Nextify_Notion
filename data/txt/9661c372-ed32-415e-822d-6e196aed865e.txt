## Idea Problem Snapshot
Pet owners struggle to find a wide range of products and services, including hotels, flights, health information, and veterinarians. This challenge is significant as it affects pet care quality and convenience. With the pet industry growing, addressing this gap is crucial for enhancing user experience.

## Idea Brainstorm Uses

- **Product Discovery**: Pet owners currently search multiple websites and apps for pet products, often leading to frustration and inefficiency.

- **Service Booking**: Users book services like pet sitting and veterinary appointments via phone calls or in-person visits, which is time-consuming.

- **Health Information Access**: Pet owners rely on general online searches or social media for health advice, lacking a centralized source for reliable information.

- **Travel Planning**: Pet owners manually research pet-friendly accommodations and travel options, often missing out on available deals or options.

- **Community Engagement**: Users engage in local pet owner groups on social media for recommendations, which can lack credibility and consistency.

- **Marketing for Businesses**: Pet-related businesses use social media and flyers to promote services, facing challenges in reaching targeted audiences effectively.

- **Client Management for Sitters**: Pet sitters manage clients through spreadsheets or informal communication, leading to disorganization and missed opportunities.

- **Veterinarian Outreach**: Veterinarians use traditional marketing methods to connect with pet owners, often resulting in low visibility and engagement.

## Market Notes
- **Growth**: The pet industry is expanding rapidly, with increasing demand for diverse products and services.

- **Budget**: Focus on cost-effective marketing strategies and lean development to maximize limited resources.

## Assumptions & Risks
- Users will prefer a single platform for pet-related services over multiple sources.
- Pet owners are willing to pay for premium listings or services.
- The demand for pet-friendly travel options is significant in Berlin.
- Pet businesses will actively engage with the platform for marketing.
- Users will trust the platform as a reliable source for health information.

## Key Risks
- Low user adoption due to existing habits of using multiple platforms.
- Competition from established pet service platforms.
- Regulatory challenges related to pet health information.
- Difficulty in acquiring a critical mass of pet businesses.
- Limited budget may hinder effective marketing and outreach.

### How RICE Was Computed
- Reach = people per week.
- Impact = 1–5 scale.
- Confidence = 0–1.
- Effort = person-weeks.
- RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort.
