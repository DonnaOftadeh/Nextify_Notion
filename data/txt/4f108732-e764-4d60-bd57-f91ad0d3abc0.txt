## Idea Problem Snapshot
Pet owners face challenges accessing comprehensive services and information related to their pets. The need for a centralized platform that includes veterinarians, grooming services, health information, and pet travel resources is pressing. This solution is essential for enhancing pet care and convenience in the Berlin region.

## Idea Brainstorm Uses

- **Service Locator**: Pet owners use Google Maps or local directories to find nearby veterinarians and grooming services, often leading to incomplete information.

- **Travel Research**: Pet travelers check multiple websites for pet-friendly accommodations, which can be time-consuming and inconsistent in policy details.

- **Social Media Groups**: Pet owners join Facebook groups or forums to seek recommendations and share experiences about services, resulting in scattered information.

- **Appointment Scheduling**: Pet sitters and service providers rely on manual calendars or messaging apps to manage bookings, leading to scheduling conflicts.

- **Health Tracking**: Pet owners use separate apps for health records and vaccination reminders, creating fragmentation in health management.

- **Community Events**: Pet owners attend local meetups or events to connect with service providers, but these opportunities are not centralized or easily accessible.

- **Referral Networks**: Veterinarians depend on word-of-mouth referrals to reach new clients, limiting their visibility and growth potential.

- **Resource Sharing**: Users exchange tips and resources through informal networks, which may lack credibility and reliability.

## Assumptions & Risks
- Users prefer a single platform for all pet-related services.
- Pet owners are willing to pay for premium features.
- Service providers will actively engage with the platform.
- Users trust online reviews and ratings for service selection.
- There is sufficient demand for pet travel resources in Berlin.

- Competition from existing pet service platforms.
- Low user adoption due to lack of awareness.
- Service providers may not see value in joining.
- Regulatory challenges in the pet care industry.
- Technology issues affecting user experience.
- Budget constraints limiting marketing efforts.

### How RICE Was Computed
- Reach = people per week.
- Impact = 1–5 scale.
- Confidence = 0–1.
- Effort = person-weeks.
- RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort.

## Synthesis Summary
