## Idea Brainstorm Uses

Pet owners in Berlin currently face challenges addressed by the proposed platform:

*   **Finding pet-friendly accommodations:** Pet owners spend hours searching multiple websites to verify pet policies and availability. Current behavior involves calling hotels directly and sifting through user reviews, which is time-consuming.
*   **Locating reliable pet sitters:** Pet owners often rely on word-of-mouth or generic online marketplaces with limited vetting. Existing behaviors involve asking friends or family for recommendations.
*   **Accessing vet information:** Finding vets specializing in certain animal types or with specific expertise can be difficult. Pet owners typically resort to general online searches or vet directories.
*   **Purchasing specialized pet products:** Finding niche pet products can require visiting multiple stores or searching various online retailers. Pet owners often settle for readily available alternatives.
*   **Planning pet travel:** Coordinating flights and transportation for pets involves navigating complex airline policies and documentation requirements. Pet owners currently spend time on multiple airline websites and pet travel forums.
*   **Connecting with local pet communities:** Finding local pet-related events or groups can be challenging, leading to isolation. Pet owners rely on social media groups or community bulletin boards.
*   **Managing pet health records:** Tracking vaccinations, medications, and medical history is often done manually. Pet owners use paper records or basic digital spreadsheets.
*   **Comparing pet insurance options:** Evaluating different pet insurance policies involves visiting multiple insurance websites. Pet owners often struggle to understand policy details and coverage.

### Early Adopter Profile

Focus on tech-savvy pet owners and businesses active on social media. They are more likely to try new platforms and provide feedback.

*   **Pet Owners:** Look for active members of online pet communities or those who frequently purchase pet products online.
*   **Pet Sitters:** Target independent pet sitters or those working through smaller agencies looking to expand their reach.
*   **Vets/Clinics:** Identify clinics with a strong online presence and a focus on client education.
*   **Pet Businesses:** Seek out local pet stores or service providers eager to try new marketing channels.

### Lightweight Market Research

*   **Surveys:** Use online tools (e.g., Google Forms, SurveyMonkey) to gather data on pet owner needs and pain points.
*   **Interviews:** Talk to local pet businesses to understand their marketing challenges and platform preferences.

## Assumptions & Risks

*   **Assumption:** Pet owners are willing to use a single platform for all their pet-related needs.
*   **Assumption:** Pet businesses (vets, groomers, hotels, etc.) will be willing to list their services on a new platform.
*   **Assumption:** A significant number of pet owners in Berlin are actively seeking new pet products and services online.
*   **Risk:** Established competitors with larger marketing budgets will make it difficult to attract users.
*   **Risk:** Building and maintaining a comprehensive and up-to-date database of pet businesses will be challenging.
*   **Risk:** Users may be hesitant to trust a new platform with sensitive information about their pets' health.

### How RICE Was Computed
- Reach = people per week.
- Impact = 1–5 scale.
- Confidence = 0–1.
- Effort = person-weeks.
- RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort.
